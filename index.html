About the Herbarium

A herbarium is a collection of preserved plant specimens and associated data used for scientific study. These specimens are typically dried, pressed, and mounted on sheets of paper, and may also include information about the plantâ€™s habitat, location, date of collection, and the name of the collector. Herbaria serve as a vital resource for botanists, ecologists, and researchers in various fields of biological science.

Importance Of Herbaria

Taxonomic Research

Herbaria are essential for the study of plant taxonomy and systematics.

They provide reference materials for identifying and classifying plants, helping botanists understand plant relationships and evolution.


Biodiversity Studies

By examining specimens from different geographical locations and time periods, researchers can assess biodiversity and track changes in plant populations.

This is crucial for conservation efforts and understanding ecological dynamics.


Historical Records

Herbaria serve as historical records of plant distribution and abundance.

They offer insights into past environments and climates, helping scientists understand how ecosystems have changed over time.
